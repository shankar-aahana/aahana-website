Why he built Aahana

Five gaps.
One practice built to close them.

After years across clinical medicine, health technology, and the skincare industry, Dr. Shankar saw the same failures everywhere in aesthetics: gaps in physician oversight, in precision, and in who actually gets seen at the highest level. Aahana was built to close every one of them.

"Most aesthetics practices treat what they can see. Precision medicine treats the person underneath."
01

The physician problem

In most practices, the physician signs the plan and someone else delivers it, sometimes without ever meeting the patient. At Aahana, Dr. Shankar leads every consultation and treatment directly. Not as a selling point. As the baseline.

02

Skin of color

Most clinical research and device testing in medical aesthetics has been conducted on lighter skin tones. Patients with deeper skin too often receive care designed for someone else. Aahana built skin of color expertise into its clinical foundation from day one.

03

LGBTQ+ care

Gay, queer, and transgender patients have specific clinical needs most practices are not trained to address. Aahana was built with this community as a primary audience, not a secondary one.

04

Gender and identity

Medical aesthetics has historically been built around a narrow idea of who its patients are. Men are among the fastest-growing and most underserved segments. Aahana is built for patients across the gender spectrum, with care that meets each where they are.

05

Precision medicine

The diagnostics available today are not being used in medical aesthetics at the level they should be. Most practices still operate on observation alone. Aahana applies the same diagnostic precision to aesthetic medicine that patients should expect from any branch of clinical care. Explore precision medicine →
