Our approach

Medicine built around
who you actually are.

Aahana was founded to close gaps that conventional medical aesthetics has left open for too long. In physician oversight. In precision. In who gets seen, heard, and cared for at the highest level.

Why Aahana exists

The industry is good at treating
what it can see.

Physicians sign off on plans someone else delivers. Treatments are chosen from menus rather than built from data. Entire categories of patients receive care that was never designed with them in mind.

That is the gap Aahana was built to close. Not with a different philosophy of aesthetics, but with a different standard of medicine.

The right patient is not defined by what they want to change. They are defined by wanting to feel on the outside the way they already feel on the inside.

Most people who walk into a medical aesthetics practice are not chasing an ideal. They are chasing alignment: wanting to look like themselves, just more fully. At Aahana, that distinction shapes every plan we build, and every conversation we have.

The gaps we are closing

Five problems.
One practice built to address them.

These are not abstract critiques. They are the specific failures of conventional medical aesthetics that shaped every decision Aahana has made.

01

The physician problem

In most practices, the physician signs the plan and someone else delivers it, sometimes without ever meeting the patient. At Aahana, Dr. Shankar leads every consultation and treatment directly. That is not a selling point. It is the baseline.

02

Skin of color

The majority of clinical research and device testing in medical aesthetics has been conducted on lighter skin tones. Patients with deeper skin receive care designed for someone else. Aahana has built skin of color expertise into its clinical foundation from the start.

03

LGBTQ+ care

Gay, queer, and transgender patients have specific clinical needs most practices are not trained to address. Aahana was built with this community as a primary audience, not a secondary one.

04

Gender and identity

Medical aesthetics has historically been built around a narrow idea of who its patients are. Men are one of the fastest-growing segments and among the most underserved. Patients across the gender spectrum deserve care that meets them where they are. Aahana is built for all of them.

05

Precision medicine

The diagnostics available today are not being used in medical aesthetics at the level they should be. Most practices still operate on observation alone. Aahana applies the same diagnostic precision to aesthetic medicine that patients should expect from any branch of clinical care.

What we believe

Aesthetics is medicine.
It deserves to be practiced that way.

The line between aesthetics and medicine is arbitrary. How we feel about how we look affects how we move through the world. A treatment that addresses hyperpigmentation or hair loss or skin laxity is clinical care with real consequences for real people.

Aahana takes that seriously. It means a physician in the room, diagnostics before treatment, and a plan that accounts for who you are, not just what you walked in asking about.

It also means honesty about what a treatment can and cannot do, and the difference between what is possible and what is merely promised.

"The goal is not to make you look different. It is to make you look like the version of yourself that already exists on the inside."
What this looks like in practice
Every patient at Aahana receives a Visia skin analysis, a full intake, and a Prakriti constitutional assessment before a single treatment is recommended. The plan is built from that data. It is sequenced, explained, and adjusted as your skin responds. Nothing is rushed. Nothing is assumed.
Innovation as a clinical obligation

The field moves fast.
Staying current is part of the job.

Dr. Shankar advises venture capital investors and healthcare AI startups, contributes to peer-reviewed research, and has built products at the intersection of microbiome science and clinical AI. That is how Aahana stays at the forefront of what is actually possible for patients.

When the evidence supports something new, Aahana integrates it. When it does not, we wait.

Patients deserve access to what is genuinely new, and protection from what is merely new-sounding.
